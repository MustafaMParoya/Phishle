What is Phishing

Phishing is a scam that targets users typically in the form of an email that seems to be from a trusted source (i.e., a bank, an ISP, or an online shopping company). They usually try to get the user to input important personal information such as driver's license information, bank account information, or social security number, so that they can get access to the user's money or online accounts.

Tips For How to Avoid and Defend Yourself from Phishing Scams

  • Avoid clicking on external links in emails unless you are certain about the contents
  • Secure accounts with multi-factor authentication
  • Make sure that the email that contacted you is from who you think it is and not a fake
  • If the email is from a large company, you can directly call the company to make sure
  • Never give your password or account information out
  • Regularly check logins and activity on accounts to make sure you have not become a victim phishing
  • If you do become a victim, contact the related companies immediately and have them lock your account

How to Detect Phishing Scams

  • Will try to get you to input important information
  • Will lure you with something free such as a coupon or refund
  • Will mimic large companies and say there is a problem with your account
