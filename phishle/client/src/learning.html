What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cybercrime tactic employed by individuals or groups to trick users into exposing sensitive information such as passwords, bank accounts, social security numbers, credit card numbers, or personal identification details. Typically initiated via email, phishing attempts often impersonate entities like banks, online retailers, or government agencies. These fraudulent messages appear legitimate and prompt recipients to click on links, download attachments, or enter confidential data on fake websites designed to resemble real ones. Once obtained, the stolen information can be used for identity theft, financial fraud, or other malicious purposes. Phishing attacks exploit human vulnerabilities rather than technical weaknesses, making awareness and caution essential defenses against falling victim to such scams..

Tips For How to Avoid and Defend Yourself from Phishing Scams

  • Avoid clicking on external links in emails unless you are certain about the contents
  • Secure accounts with multi-factor authentication
  • Make sure that the email that contacted you is from who you think it is and not a fake
  • If the email is from a large company, you can directly call the company to make sure
  • Never give your password or account information out
  • Regularly check logins and activity on accounts to make sure you have not become a victim phishing
  • If you do become a victim, contact the related companies immediately and have them lock your account

How to Detect Phishing Scams

  • Will try to get you to input important information
  • Will lure you with something free such as a coupon or refund
  • Will mimic large companies and say there is a problem with your account
